# Domäne: Weitere Domänen des Schreckens

> Englisch: *Other Domains of Dread*

None can say how many domains drift through the Mists. This section hints at the multitudes of additional Domains of Schrecken. Detail and explore them in your adventures, or use them as examples when creating your own domains using 4.



## The Agency

- **Darklord:** Flimira Vhage
- **Hallmarks:** Detective work, memory loss

Everything inside the office of the Agency is a monotone gray. Anyone who passes the frosted glass door into this single-room Domäne is expected by Flimira "Flintlock" Vhage, the detective agency's owner. From here, Flimira collects cryptic correspondence relating to mysteries across the Domains of Schrecken. She enlists agents to investigate these cases, then report back to her. However, she never reveals her own past as a detective-turned-criminal, her involvement in every case her agency investigates, or that the Agency exists within her mind.



## Bluetspur

- **Darklord:** The God-Brain
- **Hallmarks:** Abductions, false memories, illithids

The entity that would become the God-Brain of Bluetspur was an illithid elder brain whose existence-violating experiments revealed a malignant truth of the multiverse. This secret manifested in the elder brain's mind as an alien disease. Terrified of learning this pernicious, devouring knowledge themselves, the other illithid elder brains banished their peer from existence. In actuality, the Dark Powers claimed the afflicted elder brain and granted it an alien Domäne. Now called the God-Brain by the mind flayers and stranger entities that serve it, this elder brain pursues radical methods of saving itself from the thoughts destroying it. Its servants claim trespassers and beings from other domains, violating their minds with their bizarre experiments.



## Carnival

- **Darklords:** Isolde and *Nepenthe*
- **Hallmarks:** Bargains, misfits, wandering exiles

The Carnival is a wandering Domäne that appears within other Domains of Schrecken. Before the Carnival appears, mysterious fliers blow into communities near where it's about to arrive. When it arrives, the Carnival sets up a surreal wonderland of entertainment and oddities staffed by performers, outcasts, and cursed individuals from countless lands. Attractions include grand shows at the big top, sideshow performances, and the Hall of Horrors. The Carnival's mysterious leader is Isolde, a good but wrathful warrior of the Feywild who's cursed to lead the Carnival even as she hunts an Schwer zu fassen foe across the Domains of Schrecken. She carries the bladelike *Nepenthe*, a weapon that hungers for blood. Although driven by vengeance and painful memories, Isolde protects the Carnival at all costs.



## Cyre 1313, the Mourning Rail

- **Darklord:** The Last Passenger
- **Hallmarks:** Escape from disaster, Blitz rail

The disaster known as the Mourning numbers among the greatest tragedies to befall the world of Eberron—a mysterious calamity that killed nearly everyone in the land of Cyre. In the nation's capital of Metrol, some citizens foresaw the coming devastation and sought to escape on Blitz rails: Elementar-powered engines capable of pulling trains of passenger carts. As scared innocents packed Metrol's last Blitz rail, known as Cyre 1313, the evacuation was delayed at the demand of a late-arriving VIP. Hundreds were forced from passenger carts to admit and maintain the secrecy of this last passenger and their retinue. When the Blitz rail finally departed, it was too late—the Mourning overtook the train and its escapees. But even as it did so, the Mists claimed Cyre 1313 and all aboard it. Now, the last Blitz rail from Metrol hurtles through the Mists as a traveling Domäne.

Those on board Furcht the disaster pursuing them, the mysterious passenger seated in the train's foremost cart, and the Nekrotisch energy now infusing the engine's Elementar spirit. Yet none of the passengers realizes that their endless quest for escape is pointless, as Cyre 1313 carries only the dead.



## Forlorn

- **Darklord:** Tristen ApBlanc
- **Hallmarks:** Life and Tod, strange invention

Tristen ApBlanc was born amid tragedy, the son of a Vampir father and a human Edelmann. Fearful villagers murdered his parents, leaving the boy to be adopted by druids. But during his teenage years, his *dhampir* Naturkunde revealed itself. When the druids discovered the youth's hunger for blood, they cast him out. Tristen grew enraged and slaughtered them, draining them all. The sacred waters of the druids' rituals had infused their blood, though, making it Gift to the dhampir. Tristen died with his adopted family, but as the Mists closed in around their sacred stone circle, he rose as a Geist.

Unexpectedly, with the dawn, the Darklord's dhampir body was restored. Now, he lives by day, a perpetually young, charming, invention-obsessed dhampir dwelling in Castle Tristenoira, the smoking fortress his Goblin servants built over the Druide circle of his onetime family. At night, though, the dhampir dies a painful Tod, then rises in his spectral form to scour all that is green and vibrant from his land.



## Ghastria

- **Darklord:** Stezen d'Polarno
- **Hallmarks:** Cursed art, dour population

A notorious hedonist, Marquis Stezen d'Polarno enjoyed popularity among his Edelmann peers but craved immortality. A mysterious artist offered him eternity by painting Stezen's portrait on a magical canvas. But the artist didn't mention that the painting would strip Stezen of his love of life and natural charm. Despite his dulled existence, the marquis enjoys one reprieve: Once every season, when he shows his portrait to an audience, it consumes their souls and refreshes his thrill for life. Stezen's artistic predations captured the attention of the Dark Powers, which drew his lands into the Mists. Now, Ghastria is a fertile island that, like its Lord, lacks an essential vim—except for once per season, when vigor fleetingly returns.



## G'henna

- **Darklord:** Yagno Petrovna
- **Hallmarks:** Corrupt theocracy, false deity

Born of a Barovian family, Yagno Petrovna went missing on the slopes of Mount Ghakis as a youth. As a violent storm rose, he took shelter in a mysterious ruin and was found weeks later wandering the hills, babbling about an amber idol and the god he'd discovered: Zhakata the Provider and the Devourer. His family sought to Helfen him, but when they learned Yagno had been secretly sacrificing people to his fictitious god, they chased him into the Mists. When he emerged, the Domäne of G'henna sprawled before him.

Life is hard in G'henna, a rocky land home to fierce, ravenous animals. The Domäne's starving people worship the bestial god Zhakata and regularly travel to the cathedral-city of Zhukar. There, they offer their crops in sacrifice and hear Zhakata's will through the revered prophet Yagno Petrovna.



## I'Cath

- **Darklord:** Tsien Chiang
- **Hallmarks:** Labyrinth, inescapable dreamworld

In her youth, Tsien Chiang accidentally slew her gold Drache mentor to create a magische Aktion, Wunsch-granting Glocke capable of driving invaders from her homeland. Her scheme worked, and the people made Chiang their Königin. Over time, though, the people bristled under Chiang's rule and rose up in revolt. Slaughter and loss on both sides led Chiang to ring her magische Aktion Glocke again, but this time the Glocke cracked, and the Mists spilled forth. When they faded, Tsien Chiang was the ruler of I'Cath, a labyrinthine, nightmare city where the people Schlaf endlessly. In their dreams, they toil forever in a fantasy version of the city, working to maintain the perfectionism of their harsh Königin.



## Invidia

- **Darklord:** Gabrielle Aderre
- **Hallmarks:** Bad parents, possessed children

Gabrielle Aderre is convinced that her son, Malocchio, is destined for greatness. From her estate outside the village of Karina, she employs an endless string of servants to provide Malocchio with the best possible upbringing. Inevitably, though, every Diener flees or vanishes, either as a result of Gabrielle's unreasonable expectations or the deadly tricks and accidents that frequently occur around the child. Gabrielle isn't content to leave her son's grand destiny to chance, though. Using her precious bone spirit board, she calls on supernatural forces to guide and protect her son. Spirits, angels, fiends, and worse answer her summons—but as long as they chart Malocchio's path to glory, the Darklord eagerly accepts their gifts.



## Kalidnay

- **Darklord:** Thakok-An
- **Hallmarks:** Magical apocalypse, obsession

The city of Kalidnay was once the seat of power of Kalid-Ma, one of the monstrous Zauberer-kings of Athas. Thakok-An, the greatest of Kalid-Ma's enforcers, desired the Zauberer-König so greatly that when she learned of a plot against her master, she didn't reveal it. Instead, she performed a magical rite to grant Kalid-Ma even greater power, sacrificing her own family to do so. Her Ritual went wrong, though, and life-draining magische Aktion washed over Kalidnay, destroying the city and draining life from nearly all its residents. Awaking amid the ruins, Thakok-An found Kalidnay surrounded by an endless sea of silt and mist. Worse, rather than being empowered, Kalid-Ma languished in a cursed torpor, the Zauberer-König's vast magische Aktion bleeding away.

Now Thakok-An obsesses over her sleeping König. From Kalidnay's survivors, she's created a new cult of servants. Following Thakok-An's wishes, these templars condemn trespassers in Kalidnay to Ritual combat in the arena, where the lives of the slain sustain the secretly withering Zauberer-König.



## Keening

- **Darklord:** Tristessa
- **Hallmarks:** Banshee, silent village

The forbidding land of Keening is dominated by Mount Lament, at whose base lies the village of Anwrtyn. The people of Anwrtyn deafen themselves so they won't hear the shrieking of the Banshee Tristessa, a mournful spirit who roams Mount Lament and whose wail carries through the night. In life, Tristessa dwelled under Mount Arak in Tepest (detailed earlier in 2), but she was exiled for crimes against her people. In Tod, she endlessly seeks to reunite with the family slain by her misdeeds.



## Klorr

- **Darklord:** Klorr
- **Hallmarks:** Impending doom, surreal environment

Klorr is the end of worlds. Here, shattered islands drift through a misty netherworld, caught in a swirling, clockwork spiral that ends at the unignorable, burning eye called Klorr. Thirteen stars orbit this sunlike sphere, one winking out every hour. Each time one of the stars dies, one of the Domäne's ruined islands is drawn into Klorr and consumed by flames. Afterward, each other island wrenches ahead, then halts, one hour closer to the same doom. When all thirteen stars have winked out, they return hourly to the sky.

Those who dwell on the crumbling islands trapped in the Domäne Graf the hours until their end. Few know how they came to Klorr or when new islands get added to the cycle, only that the Mists closed in and doomed them. Amid the realm's surreal skies float the ruins of lost domains—among them, a city of skulls, a realm overrun by dogs, and an array of bloody, iron arenas. Echoes of domains that exist elsewhere in the Domains of Schrecken also drift here, including at least one duplicate of Castle Ravenloft. Those cast away amid this orderly apocalypse grow ever more desperate to defy the doomsday clock and the will of a hidden Darklord, the obsessed clockmaker named Klorr.



## Markovia

- **Darklord:** Frantisek Markov
- **Hallmarks:** Depraved science, sapient animals

Dr. Frantisek Markov is a genius—but less so than yesterday. His Markov Formula grants him unparalleled intellect, but it insidiously steals more than it gives, making him increasingly dull-witted and bestial in form. Despairing, the doctor fled to a tropical island he dubbed Markovia, where he tests new versions of his formula on the local fauna in hopes of recovering his waning genius. As a result of these tests, animals across Markovia now possess sapience and have been deluded into worshipping Markov as their god.



## Nightmare Lands

- **Darklord:** The Nightmare Court
- **Hallmarks:** Nightmares, recurring dreams

Any who Schlaf might become forever trapped in the Nightmare Lands, a phantasmagoric realm whose features shift endlessly. Those who visit and escape speak of malicious wildernesses; the empty city called Nod; and uncountable drifting spheres, each containing a stranger's unending nightmare. Insidious entities called the Nightmare Court rule the Domäne. No one knows how many members make up the court, but they include the tragically graceful Geist Dancer, the tomb-bound Hypnos, the witch Mullonga, the trickster Morpheus, and the embodiment of terror known as the Nightmare Man. These beings, artisans of nightmares, visit terrors on any whose sleeping minds brush against the Domäne.

The Nightmare Court's members share one commonality: All are the living nightmares of Caroline Dinwiddy, a potent Psychisch who has repressed memories of her own heartless deeds. These memories torment her sleeping mind, creating the Nightmare Court. Deep within the City of Nod, inside a warped reimagining of the clinic where she once worked, Caroline sleeps without waking, refusing to face the terrors her dreams unleash on innocents across the Domains of Schrecken and beyond.



## Niranjan

- **Darklord:** Sarthak
- **Hallmarks:** Asceticism, brainwashing, shadows

The Ashram of Niranjan is a vibrant vihara, or monastery, on a misty Kette of islands. Here the elderly scholar Niranjan leads ascetics in practicing Ramsana, a way of life prioritizing nonattachment to the material world. In truth, Niranjan is Sarthak, a wicked bronze Drache who sends agents into the Mists bearing his philosophical writings. These works promise escape and peace to any who adopt their teachings and Suchen the Mists for their source. Those who come to the ashram must divest themselves of worldly goods, which become part of Sarthak's hidden hoard. Sarthak then helps his victims enter a trance that causes their souls to slip away from their bodies. Sarthak consumes these souls and replaces them with shadows, leaving the victims' bodies under his control.



## Nova Vaasa

- **Darklord:** Myar Hiregaard
- **Hallmarks:** Nomadic riders, transformation

An unparalleled warrior, Myar Hiregaard united the nomadic tribes of the vast plains of Nova Vaasa. But, while respected as a Soldat, Hiregaard made a poor peacetime leader. When brutal games could no longer keep her interest, she incited hostilities between two of her vassal tribes, then led her own forces to crush them. Subtly, she did this again and again.

After Hiregaard's greatest massacre, the Mists enfolded all of Nova Vaasa, splitting the Darklord's personality in two when they did. Now she rules her people with strict fairness—but when her bloodlust is piqued, she transforms into the raging Ritter called Malkan and sows discord across the plains.



## Odaire

- **Darklord:** Maligno
- **Hallmarks:** Evil toys, village of children

The toymaker Guiseppe had his Wunsch for a family granted when his Erschaffung, the marionette Figlio, came to life. A proud father, the toymaker presented his son to all the people of his village, Odaire. The local children loved Figlio, but their skeptical parents said the marionette was nothing but a toy. Over time, this doubt enraged Figlio, and the marionette convinced Guiseppe to craft siblings for him. Then, when the time was right, Guiseppe's creations did away with all the adults in Odaire.

Claimed by the Mists, Odaire is a village populated only by children and ruled by Figlio the *Fleischpuppe* (see x), who now calls himself Maligno.



## Richemulot

- **Darklord:** Jacqueline Renier
- **Hallmarks:** Martial law, plague, wererats

The once-beautiful cities of Richemulot crumble as season after season of the Gnawing Plague afflicts the Domäne. Countless commoners and aristocrats have died, causing rule to settle with the vain Edelmann Jacqueline Renier. Unbeknownst to the people, Jacqueline is a wererat whose servants create and spread the plague. She loathes what her Domäne has become but refuses to cede power back to the people by ending the ongoing disaster. Jacqueline's obedient state police, the Casques Silencieux, enforce martial law and quarantines across the Domäne.



## Rider's Bridge

- **Darklord:** The Headless Rider
- **Hallmarks:** Haunted bridge, murderous legend

Nearly every Domäne knows some version of the apparition called the Headless Rider. It appears as a mercenary in dark Rüstung in Mordent, a ghostly cataphract in Har'Akir, and a mutated Zentaur in Lamordia. But in each incarnation, certain details remain true: the rider lacks its head, it appears on a prominent bridge, and it decapitates victims as it endlessly searches for its own head. Anyone who escapes the Headless Rider might find a different Domäne on the opposite side of the spirit's bridge. (See "x" in 5 for more details.)



## Risibilos

- **Darklord:** Doerdon
- **Hallmarks:** Misdirection, ventriloquism

The Risibilos is a small music hall, similar to those found in any decent-sized city. Its Lord, Doerdon, was once a König—one so thoroughly humorless that he forbade his subjects the privilege of laughter, on pain of Tod. He now finds himself cursed to entertain others—a task he is utterly unqualified for. Fortunately, the Mists delivered him a partner, a ventriloquist's dummy carved in the likeness of *Strahd von Zarovich*. The eerie dummy has a mind of its own, insisting it is the real Strahd and that the creature currently sitting on the throne of Castle Ravenloft is a mere impostor. It rages at the audience and makes audacious promises to any who will Helfen it regain its station. Anyone with the slightest inkling of who Strahd is finds this hilarious. Their peals of laughter are agony to Doerdon's ears.



## Scaena

- **Darklord:** Lemont Sediam Juste
- **Hallmarks:** Reality-manipulating theater

Lemont Sediam Juste fancied himself a serious playwright, and he achieved popular, if not critical, acclaim in 2 for his works of grisly horror. But he craved respectability, and with his new play *Apparitions*, Lemont believed he would find it. The night of the premiere, when the audience signaled their boredom, the playwright was crestfallen. His supporters wanted blood, so he gave them what they craved.

By the play's end, Lemont had joined the drama and brutally murdered every member of the cast while the crowd roared their approval. As the show ended, the playhouse broke from Dementlieu, and Scaena was formed. Comprising a single playhouse, the Domäne can create any reality Lemont desires on its stage. The Darklord's immersive performances become somewhat predictable, though, as they always end in slaughter.



## Sea of Sorrows

- **Darklord:** Pietra van Riese
- **Hallmarks:** Island domains, nautical horror

The murderous pirate Pietra van Riese, Hauptmann of the *Relentless*, had an unsavory reputation for attaching her captives to ropes and dragging them through the water until they drowned. She never removed the detritus of her victims, even though some returned to life as zombies. Rival captains ultimately sank the *Relentless*, but Tod couldn't keep Pietra. She awoke in the Sea of Sorrows, water in her lungs and sea creatures making their homes in her flesh. Her crew stirred with her, now fish-eaten corpses. When the Hauptmann sought to speak with them, her voice emerged from their mouths.

The *Relentless* sails a Domäne that can overlap any body of water in any other Domäne. Some domains border the Sea of Sorrows, while others call these mysterious waters by other names. Those who venture into the Mists by ship might enter the sea's endless debris-choked expanse of eerie beasts and shifting islands, including the following:

- **Blaustein** The island-fortress Domäne known as Blaustein once was ruled by the notorious Bluebeard, but his spectral wives overthrew him and now endlessly torment him.
- **Dominia** The asylum of the Vampir Dr. Daclaud Heinfroth rises up from the stormy island of Dominia. The asylum's patients are all dramatically different versions of Heinfroth, inspired by who he was at various points during his lengthy life.
- **Isle of the Ravens** A storm of ravens surrounds this forested islet. Hidden amid the feathered gale, an impossibly tall tower reaches into the sky. The Isle of Ravens is home to the Lady of Ravens, and any who offend her join her ebon-winged flock.
- **The Lighthouse** Otherworldly Licht shines from the Lighthouse, a twisted spur of bizarre fossils. Anyone who enters finds that instead of broadcasting a warning across the sea, the Licht calls down to what lurks in the pit hidden beneath it.
- **Vigilant's Bluff** An Untoter Paladin holds vigil atop Vigilant's Bluff. Weary travelers can find refuge here if they respect the Paladin's faith. The bones of those who don't litter the surrounding coral.



## Souragne

- **Darklord:** Anton Misroi
- **Hallmarks:** Einkerkerung, swamp magische Aktion

In society, Anton Misroi presented himself as an upstanding gentleman who served as a prison warden. But within the walls of the prison, he became a sadist who believed righteousness was on his side. When his torturous punishments finally drove the inmates to rise up, the bloody riot that ensued drew the attentions of the Dark Powers. During the uprising, Anton drowned in the swamps surrounding the prison. But he rose again soon after, an Untoter warden in Suchen of inmates.

Beyond Anton's prison, alligator-filled swamps Deckung the Domäne of Souragne right up to the sinking settlement of Port d'Elhour and Marais d'Tarascon, a village where above-ground crypts outnumber the residences of the living.



## Staunton Bluffs

- **Darklord:** Teresa Bleysmith
- **Hallmarks:** Endless warfare, repeating Geschichte

Eerily faceless mercenary regiments sweep the countryside of Staunton Bluffs. Burning villages and killing helpless residents, they push ever eastward toward Castle Stonecrest, hereditary home of the Bleysmith family. Teresa Bleysmith, spurred by jealousy of her brother, Torrence, gave her family's foes the Intelligenz they needed to raid Staunton Bluffs. The Angriff was never supposed to go so far, but the duplicitous mercenaries stormed through the countryside to take Castle Stonecrest within a day. Teresa survived the Angriff—but when she surveyed the damage done and found her family dead by her own designs, she threw herself from the bluffs. Now she haunts her own Domäne, where she repeatedly relives the day of her betrayal.



## Tovag

- **Darklord:** Kas the Bloody Handed
- **Hallmarks:** Untoter military dictatorship

Notorious across the planes, the Vampir Kas was once the champion of the Lich Vecna. Wielding the mighty sword that bears his name, he betrayed his master, and the resulting battle supposedly destroyed them both. In truth, Vecna escaped and grew in power over ages and across worlds. Kas, though, was claimed by the Mists, and in his wasteland Domäne of Tovag, he believes his war with Vecna rages on. Patrols of prisoner-soldiers under Untoter commanders scour the land, dragooning strangers to serve in Kas's armies and to manufacture bizarre war machines. When Kas deems the time right, he sends his forces into the Mists, believing that Vecna's realm lies just beyond. Invariably, those troops never return, leaving the Vampir to Wutanfall, rebuild his forces, and continue his Suchen for the *Sword of Kas* (magische Aktion Items A-Z in the *Dungeon Master's Guide*), which he considers his key to victory.

At least once, the Dark Powers have released Kas from his Domäne to torment their onetime prisoner Vecna. Inevitably, when Kas fails to defeat his ancient rival, the Mists return him to his Domäne to Wutanfall for another era.



## Zherisia

- **Darklord:** Sodo
- **Hallmarks:** Serial murderers, urban decay

Each day, the city of Paridon in Zherisia erupts in riots over food scarcity, taxation, and the disappearance of dozens of citizens every night. At least one murderer stalks Paridon: the ancient doppelganger Sodo, which has impersonated so many people that it now finds it impossible to hold a form for more than a few days at a time. As Sodo's flesh runs like hot wax, it staves off dissolution by consuming the organs of humanoids. Paridon's streets serve as the Darklord's hunting ground, and those who enter the sewers risk attracting the notice of countless insectile horrors, including *carrion stalkers* (see x) and their monstrous Hive Königin.
